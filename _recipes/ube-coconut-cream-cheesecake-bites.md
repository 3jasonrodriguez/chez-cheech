## Ingredients

**Coconut Crumble**
- 3 whole graham crackers, crushed
- 1/2 cup unsweetened coconut flakes, toasted (split: crumble + topping)
- 1/4 stick unsalted butter, melted (~2 tbsp)
- 1 1/2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/8 tsp fine sea salt
- Note: ran slightly short on crumble for all 12 cavities

**Ube Cheesecake**
- 8 oz cream cheese, softened
- 6 tbsp powdered sugar
- 1/4 cup ube halaya jam + 1 tbsp more, added before chilling to boost flavor
- 1/2 tsp ube extract
- 1/2 cup heavy cream, ice cold
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/8 tsp fine sea salt
- Note: base wasn't as ube-forward as expected even after original amounts — extra tbsp of jam added right before chilling

**Coconut Cream Swirl**
- 1/2 cup heavy cream, ice cold
- 5 tbsp canned coconut cream, chilled (3 tbsp original + 2 tbsp extra added)
- 1 1/2 tbsp powdered sugar
- 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
- 4 tsp ube extract, added to bring ube flavor forward through the swirl since the base fell short

## Steps

1. Lightly grease all 12 mini muffin cavities (no liners).
2. Toast coconut flakes in a dry pan, ~4 min, until golden. Set aside 1/3 for topping.
3. Mix graham crumbs, most of the toasted coconut, melted butter, brown sugar, cinnamon, salt. Squeeze test for clump-but-crumbles texture.
4. Press a thin layer of crumble into each cavity.
5. Freeze tray flat, 15 min.
6. Beat cream cheese, powdered sugar, ube jam, ube extract, vanilla, salt until smooth.
7. Whip heavy cream to stiff peaks, fold gently into ube mixture.
8. Pipe cheesecake layer over crumble. Chilled overnight instead of 3 hours — texture came out noticeably firmer.
9. Whip heavy cream, coconut cream, powdered sugar, vanilla, and ube extract to soft peaks.
10. Pipe swirl on top of set cheesecake bites.
11. Sprinkle reserved toasted coconut. Run a knife around edge before popping out.
